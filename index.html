Literature Survey

These days, automated farming systems, crop monitoring, greenhouse cultivation, and other agricultural industries make substantial use of precision agriculture and IoT technologies. In recent years, there has been a notable surge in the adoption of smart agricultural technologies due to the growing desire for efficient and sustainable farming methods.

The global vanilla market faces numerous challenges, including quality inconsistency, yield variability, and labor-intensive cultivation processes. As a result, there is a growing need for automated systems and intelligent monitoring solutions in vanilla production. The world has many approaches to precision agriculture, but this research focuses specifically on greenhouse-based vanilla cultivation using IoT and machine learning technologies.

The study focuses on using automated methods to maintain ideal growing conditions, monitor environmental factors, identify growth stages in vanilla beans, and evaluate bean quality. IoT sensors, environmental control systems, and YOLOv8 object detection algorithms are integrated to offer a complete solution for sustainable vanilla production. This strategy tackles important issues in vanilla cultivation, specifically in the context of Sri Lankan agriculture, such as precise growth forecasting, quality evaluation, and automated environmental management.

In order to improve production efficiency and bean quality while lowering resource consumption and manual intervention, the suggested system makes use of cutting-edge technology to monitor and optimize a variety of aspects of vanilla cultivation, from environmental conditions to disease detection.

Research Gap

The study of market value analysis, disease detection, growth detection, and effective greenhouse selection and management fills in a number of important gaps in the current economic and agricultural models for vanilla production. Due to a lack of predictive methods for monitoring development phases, disease onset, and market trends, vanilla growers currently confront difficulties that frequently lead to less than ideal yields and lower profit margins. Conventional techniques for tracking development and detecting illnesses are usually labor-intensive and prone to errors, which causes intervention delays and inconsistent bean quality. Global environmental and economic factors also affect vanilla market swings, but few studies use predictive modeling to assist growers in navigating these shifts.

Furthermore, although greenhouse settings are frequently employed in the production of vanilla, they are frequently not tailored to the type of climate that vanilla requires, which results in inefficient use of resources and decreased crop yields. Real-time monitoring and adaptive climate management that meet the unique growth and quality requirements of vanilla are rarely included in current greenhouse technology, depriving growers of focused insights to increase output and operational effectiveness. By fusing developments in economic forecasting, machine learning, and the Internet of Things, this study aims to bridge these gaps by creating a holistic, data-driven solution for market analysis, illness detection, growth projection, and greenhouse management optimization.

Methodology

By combining modern technology and in-depth market research, the Precision Agriculture system with centralised IoT-enabled Greenhouse Management for sustainable Vanilla Production aims to transform vanilla agriculture in Sri Lanka. This system's main objective is to forecast farmer revenues in the future using historical and current vanilla production data, giving farmers useful information they can use. The procedure starts with careful data preparation, which entails gathering and compiling pertinent data into databases and CSV files. After that, machine learning models are created using this data to forecast important outcomes like vanilla yield, quality, and market prices

The iterative process of the system involves testing and predicting the model, then rigorously validating and optimising it to guarantee the precision and dependability of the forecasts. The models are used to generate ongoing predictions once they have been adjusted. In addition to predicting salaries, the algorithm also projects future net worth and the demand for vanilla globally, providing users with a thorough understanding of market trends. Making educated decisions regarding market tactics, resource allocation, and farming techniques requires the use of this knowledge. The system's centralised IoT-enabled greenhouse management feature further improves the sustainability and financial viability of Sri Lankan vanilla production by guaranteeing ideal growing conditions through real-time environmental parameter monitoring.

Staircase detection is the main part of this research because other components depend on this component. There are different approaches to detect objects. In our scenario, we have used the vision-based method. This method is done by using the deep learning image classification technique. We have used VGG-16 and YOLO image processing models for our further development.

Gather distance is the second main part of this research because other dimensions calculations depend on distance. The Nao robot’s sonar sensor was used to calculate the distance. Using that sonar sensor, we can get the actual distance between the robot and the staircase.

Initially, we found out how to extract human motion from human walking, so we used a walking pattern generator to consider human characteristics (Walking Circle, Step length, Heel clearance, foot position) we had used here as a position-based approach. So, we have to implement a walking pattern generator and we have given here actual human data on walking and balancing the body, then when the robot starts to walk there is a feedback controller and it will compare the existing data in the generator and the current data of the robot. So, after that, if the current data is not in the possible range balance controller will balance the robot’s movement after the kinematic operation has been done. When the climbing staircase robot has to move upward its leg than the usual range, it will be detected by the feedback controller and the upper body will be controlled to bend forward, and we used the knee-ankle strategy to implement this climbing staircase in our proposed system.
